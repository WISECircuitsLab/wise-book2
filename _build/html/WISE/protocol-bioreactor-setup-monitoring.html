Bioreactor Setup and Monitoring Protocol

Bioreactor Assembly + Sterilization#

NOTE: Use heat proof gloves when handling anything coming out of the autoclave. Items may look cool to the touch but will burn you!

  1. First assemble the bioreactor using the pictures below.
  2. Place the black rubber o-rings on each end of the column to prevent liquid leakage. Ensure the shape of the o-ring fits within the ridges on the column (Fig 1).
  3. Use the two 6-inch clamps to connect the cap and domed top to the column (Fig 2).
  4. Attach the electrode holder through the center port and ensure it is secured.
  5. Add the magnetic stir bar into the bioreactor through the central port. (This needs to be autoclaved inside the bioreactor).
  6. Use aluminum foil to cover all ports on the domed cap.
  7. Using the sterile secondary container (plastic box labelled “sterile secondary container”), transfer the bioreactor to Photonics, floor 6.
  8. Next sterilize the bioreactor.

    Ensure you have received autoclave training from someone in the Jimenez or WISE Lab before proceeding with the next steps.

  9. Place the bioreactor in an autoclave bin.
  10. Ensure all ports are covered with aluminum foil.
  11. Place autoclave tape on all ports and use it to secure the aluminum foil covering.
  12. Open the autoclave using the “open door” button.
    1. If there is already autoclaved material in the autoclave, use the heat proof gloves and carefully remove the autoclave bin with the samples inside. There is usually a table or cart you can set them on.
  13. Carefully place the autoclave bin with the bioreactor into the autoclave (can use either shelf).
  14. Close the door by pressing “close door” - you may need to manually lift the door as well to ensure it is completely closed.
  15. Select “cycle select”.
  16. Select “gravity”.
  17. Select the 30 min gravity cycle setting.
  18. When prompted, click “start cycle”.
  19. Wait by the autoclave for approximately 2-5 minutes until the gasket pressure comes to pressure and the cycle starts.
    1. You will see an “abort cycle” button at the bottom of the screen, this means the cycle has started!

    Gravity cycles typically take around 1 hour, you can come back later in the day to remove it from the autoclave.

    Because the autoclave is hot and the bioreactor is metal, it gets EXTREMELY hot and will burn you if touched without heat proof gloves. Use heat proof gloves and let cool at room temperature for approximately 1 hour before handling without gloves.

  20. Transfer the bioreactor to WISE Lab in PHO, or keep in CILSE until needed.

Bioreactor Inoculation with Media and Cells#

  1. NOTE: As of 2/18 inoculation will occur in WISE Lab (PHO 303), this may change as we set up in the Jimenez lab.
  2. First ensure sufficient media (3L) and liquid cell cultures have been prepared.
  3. Spray the entire bioreactor (concentrating on the ports) with 70% ethanol to sterilize the surface.
  4. Loosen the cap of the 1L media bottle and loosen the aluminum foil covering the center port.
  5. Carefully pour the media from the bottle into the bioreactor without touching the bioreactor with the lip of the bottle.
  6. Repeat steps 3-4 with the second bottle of media.
  7. Cover the port with aluminum foil and spray with ethanol.
  8. When ready to inoculate, first measure the OD of the liquid culture and note this in the experiment documentation.
  9. Calculate the amount of culture to add. Add enough of the liquid culture to the 2L of media to obtain a starting OD of 0.1. This will vary depending on the OD of the liquid culture used.
    • Think of this calculation as a dilution of the liquid culture using 2L of media.
  10. Spray the port with 70% ethanol.
  11. Loosen the cap of the falcon tube with culture and loosen the aluminum foil covering the center port.
  12. Pour the culture into the bioreactor being careful not to touch the lip of the tube to the bioreactor.
  13. Cover the port with aluminum foil and spray with 70% ethanol.

Heating and Air Circulation#

  1. Put gloves on.
  2. Place the bioreactor inoculated with media and cells onto the heating and stir plate.
    1. The bioreactor should already have a magnetic stir bar inside.
  3. Sterilize tubing for air circulation and pump.
    1. Make a 10% bleach solution using a large beaker.
    2. Insert the air tubing and the small pump tubing into the bleach solution and let sit for 15 minutes.
      • Ensure that the inner lining of the tubing is in contact with the bleach.
  4. Connect the two tubings together (there is a smaller one which fits into the larger one).
  5. Connect the larger tubing to the air line and point the open end of the tube into the sink.
  6. Turn on the air for approximately 1 minute or until all the liquid is expelled from the tube. Turn off air.
  7. Spray one of the side ports with 70% ethanol, remove the aluminum foil, and place the tube inside.
  8. Cover the port with the aluminum foil and spray with ethanol again.
  9. Turn on the air and observe from the top port if bubbling is occurring. Turn off air until the end of the set up.
    1. See picture below of how much bubbling is correct for Marinobacter.
    2. If using a new strain check with the bio team lead.
  10. Spray the top port with ethanol (the port with the electrode holder). Peel back the aluminum foil. Spray the electrode connector with ethanol and let dry for 1 min.
  11. Carefully insert the connector with the electrode into the electrode holder. And ensure that ONLY the electrode is submerged in the liquid.
  12. Cover the port with aluminum foil and spray again with ethanol.
  13. For the pump tubing, connect the pump to the tubing and turn the pump on to get rid of any leftover bleach in the tubing.
  14. Spray the unused side port with ethanol. Peel back the aluminum foil. Spray the tubing with ethanol and let dry for 1 min.
  15. Insert the “input” end of the tubing into the bioreactor using the unused side port and cover with aluminum foil. Leave the “output” end of the tubing in an empty falcon tube.
  16. Turn on heating to 30°C for Marinobacter, and stirring to 120 rpm for Marinobacter.
    • Settings may change depending on strain of bacteria in use. Check with bio lead.
  17. Turn on air and ensure bubbling is occurring.

OD Monitoring#

  1. Fill an empty cuvette with 1mL of plain media (BB for Marinobacter) using a 1000uL pipette.
  2. Fill another empty cuvette with 900uL of plain media (BB for Marinobacter) using a 1000uL pipette.
  3. Start the pump to waste approximately 200uL of culture (approximately 1 min) into a falcon tube. Turn the pump off.
  4. Take 100uL of the culture and add it to the cuvette with 900uL of plain media.
  5. Turn on the OD reader.
  6. Insert the cuvette with plain media and measure the blank.
  7. Remove the cuvette and insert the cuvette with media and cells and measure the sample’s OD.
  8. Multiply the number shown by 10, this is the OD of the bioreactor. Mark the time and the OD in the experiment documentation.
  9. Perform an OD measurement 2x a day.
